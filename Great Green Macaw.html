Great Green Macaw

Also known as the Buffon’s macaw or the great military macaw, the great green macaw is the world’s third-biggest macaw species. They reach 33.5 to 35.5 inches with a wingspan of 44 to 50 inches. They’re also the second heaviest macaw after the hyacinth macaw, weighing around 1.3 kg.

Despite their size, their tails are much shorter than other macaws. Great green macaws are also commonly mistaken for military macaws because they share similar markings and colorings – and they’re both referred to as a “military” bird, which adds to the confusion. However, great green macaws are much larger than military parrots.
